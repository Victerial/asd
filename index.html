Founder

Christian von Koenigsegg, a visionary entrepreneur and automotive engineer, is the founder of Koenigsegg Automotive AB, a Swedish high-performance sports car manufacturer. Born on July 2, 1972, in Stockholm, Sweden, Koenigsegg displayed an early passion for engineering and automobiles. In 1994, at the age of 22, he founded Koenigsegg with the goal of creating some of the world's most technologically advanced and exhilarating hypercars. Known for his relentless pursuit of innovation, Koenigsegg has played a pivotal role in shaping the automotive industry, consistently pushing the boundaries of what is possible in terms of speed, performance, and cutting-edge technology. His commitment to excellence and his ability to blend artistry with engineering have established Koenigsegg as a symbol of automotive brilliance.

Transmission

The Koenigsegg Jesko features an all-new 9-speed, multi-clutch transmission – 100% designed and built in-house at Koenigsegg. The Light Speed Transmission (LST) represents a scale of evolution not seen since the development of modern dual-clutch technology at the turn of the century. LST comprises nine forward gears and seven wet, multidisc clutches in a compact, ultra-light package. The LST is capable of upward and downward gear changes between gears at near light speed thanks to the simultaneous opening and closing of clutches that allow for totally seamless acceleration or deceleration. That LST gear changes can occur in virtually zero time between adjacent gears is impressive. But what takes LST from impressive to evolutionary, however, is that these shift-time benefits apply when changing between ANY forward gears.
